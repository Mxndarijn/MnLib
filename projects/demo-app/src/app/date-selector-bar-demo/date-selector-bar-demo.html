Date Selector Bar Demo

A day strip with a "Today" button, a month header and arrows. Given room the strip runs Monday to Sunday, so the weekday columns hold still as you page; as the bar narrows it shows fewer days rather than adding a row. Selecting a day or pressing "Today" emits it; the arrows page the strip and never change the selection. Where the strip crosses into a new month, the days either side of the break move apart. Narrower still, and the days give way to a date picker beside Today.

Default

Uncustomised, with the selection kept in the parent through [selectedDate] and (dateSelected).

Sizes to its container

The bar measures itself, not the window. Drag the handle in the bottom-right corner of the frame: days drop away one at a time, and the bar stays a single row throughout — it never stacks. A full seven days is Monday-aligned; below that there is no week to align to, so the strip slides to keep the selected day in view. Under 478px, where even three days won't fit, it hands over to the date picker.

The window stays put

Picking a day that's already on the strip leaves the tiles exactly where they are. These buttons move [selectedDate] from outside the bar, the way a month view or a deep link would: step a day at a time past the edge and the window slides by one, jump three weeks and it gives up on scrolling and lands centred on the new day. Move the window with the arrows instead and the selection stays behind — that's the one way to look around without committing to a day.

Focus a day tile and use the left and right arrow keys to walk the strip. At either end the window steps one day at a time, so the days read as one continuous run rather than separate pages. Home and End jump to the ends of the visible window.

What the bar emits

(dateSelected) fires only when the day actually changes. Re-selecting the current tile, or pressing "Today" while today is already selected, stays silent so consumers don't reload for nothing. The arrows never emit.
